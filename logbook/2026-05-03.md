## Soldering Oxidation

Soldering iron tip oxidized after plastic-cap incident, solder wouldn't wet,
formed balls at the end of the wire instead of flowing. The color was smilar to
purple and black. Swapped the tip, immediately better.

Lesson: tin the tip on heat-up and on cool-down, don't leave it bare at
temperature. Don't leave the iron heated up on air; 

```
iron oxidizes when heated in air without solder protecting it. A clean iron tip
should always be coated in a thin film of solder ("tinned"), which forms a
barrier between the iron and oxygen. The oxide layer is purplish-blackish,
doesn't conduct heat well, and solder will not wet to it."
```

Remember to always tin the tip again before turning the iron off. Clean, then
add fresh solder to coat it. The tip cools down covered in solder, oxide can't
form, and next session the tip is in good condition.
